# Content Pillars

## Distribution Targets

| Pillar | Target | Focus |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Technology Strategy | 30% | Architecture, tech choices, scaling |
| Leadership & Management | 25% | Team building, hiring, 1:1s, culture |
| Execution & Delivery | 20% | Shipping, process, sprints, velocity |
| Founder Lessons | 15% | Personal experience, failures, pivots |
| Market & AI Trends | 10% | Industry commentary, AI applications |

## Pillar Definitions

### technology-strategy (30%)

Content about making technical decisions at the strategic level.

**Topics:**
- Architecture decisions and trade-offs
- Build vs buy analysis
- Technical debt management
- Platform and infrastructure choices
- Scaling strategies
- Technology evaluation frameworks

**Target audience:** CTOs, Tech Leads, Engineering Managers making architectural decisions.

**Example titles:**
- "The 3-Question Test for Every Architecture Decision"
- "When to Kill a Microservice"
- "Technical Debt Isn't Your Problemâ€”Denial Is"

### leadership-management (25%)

Content about leading and developing engineering teams.

**Topics:**
- Hiring and interviewing
- 1:1 structures and feedback
- Team culture and values
- Performance management
- Career development
- Cross-functional collaboration

**Target audience:** Engineering Managers, new CTOs, Tech Leads moving into management.

**Example titles:**
- "The Hire I Almost Didn't Make"
- "My 1:1 Template After 50 Direct Reports"
- "When Your Best Engineer Wants to Leave"

### execution-delivery (20%)

Content about shipping software effectively.

**Topics:**
- Sprint planning and estimation
- Velocity and throughput
- Deployment and release strategies
- Quality and testing approaches
- Technical project management
- Incident response

**Target audience:** Engineering Managers, Tech Leads, developers moving to lead roles.

**Example titles:**
- "Why Your Estimates Are Wrong (And That's Fine)"
- "The Deploy Checklist That Saved Us"
- "Stop Measuring Velocity, Start Measuring Flow"

### founder-lessons (15%)

Personal stories and lessons from building companies.

**Topics:**
- Founder-to-CTO transitions
- Startup mistakes and pivots
- Fundraising and board dynamics
- Co-founder relationships
- Burnout and sustainability
- First-hand failure stories

**Target audience:** Technical founders, first-time CTOs, aspiring founders.

**Example titles:**
- "The Six Months I Wasted Building the Wrong Thing"
- "What My First Board Meeting Taught Me"
- "I Fired Myself as CTO"

### market-ai-trends (10%)

Commentary on industry trends and AI developments.

**Topics:**
- AI tool adoption in engineering
- Market shifts affecting tech strategy
- Emerging technologies worth watching
- Industry pattern analysis
- AI implementation case studies

**Target audience:** CTOs and leaders wanting to stay current.

**Example titles:**
- "What 50 Startup Tech Stacks Taught Me About 2024"
- "The AI Tools Actually Worth Your Engineering Time"
- "Why Most AI Implementations Fail"

## Tracking Distribution

When reviewing the pipeline:

1. Count ideas by pillar (all statuses except `archived`)
2. Calculate percentage vs targets
3. Flag pillars that are:
   - Over by >10%: Pause new ideas in this pillar
   - Under by >10%: Prioritise ideation here

## Rebalancing Strategy

If distribution is off:

- **technology-strategy over:** Wait; it's the core pillar
- **leadership-management under:** Draw from recent team experiences
- **execution-delivery under:** Mine past projects for process learnings
- **founder-lessons under:** Schedule reflection time; personal stories need headspace
- **market-ai-trends over:** Reduce; this pillar ages fastest
